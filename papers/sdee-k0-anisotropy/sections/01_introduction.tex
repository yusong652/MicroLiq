\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Soil liquefaction resistance under cyclic loading is influenced by both density state and stress anisotropy.
While conventional triaxial tests provide valuable insights, hollow cylinder apparatus (HCA) tests are better suited to represent stress paths involving rotation of principal stress axes.

This manuscript investigates the influence of initial lateral-to-vertical stress ratio \Kzero{} on liquefaction resistance using discrete element method (DEM) simulations of undrained cyclic torsional shear in HCA specimens.
The study combines macroscopic response analysis (e.g., cyclic resistance, excess pore pressure, stiffness degradation) with microscopic fabric descriptors (e.g., coordination number and fabric anisotropy).

The main objectives are:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item to quantify the effect of initial stress anisotropy on liquefaction resistance under cyclic torsional shear,
  \item to evaluate whether energy-based and stiffness-based macroscopic indicators can explain observed differences, and
  \item to identify microscopic fabric mechanisms associated with \Kzero-dependent behavior.
\end{enumerate}
